Quick Answers

  • Driver License

    • How do I renew my driver license?

      Go to a driver license station with your proof of legal presence and current license, fill application form there, take eye test, and pay fees.

    • Where do I go to apply for a driver license?

      The main Driver Licensing stations are at Kalihi-Kapalama, Kapolei, Koolau, Wahiawa, and Waianae.

    • How do I replace a lost driver's license?

      To replace a lost, stolen, or damaged Hawaii driver license, go to a driver license station.

    • How do I get a Hawai`i driver license for the first time?

      Go to a driver license station with your proof of legal presence, take a written exam there along with an eye test, photographs, and fingerprints, pay fees, and schedule a road test appointment.

  • Motor Vehicle

    • How do I transfer ownership of my vehicle or motorcycle?

      To transfer ownership of a vehicle or motorcycle, both the buyer and the seller need to fill out portions of the vehicle's Certificate of Title.

    • How do I register a brand new motor vehicle?

      To register a new motor vehicle, apply by mail or in person at any Satellite City Hall.

    • How do I register a new trailer?

      Register your trailer by mail or in person at a Satellite City Hall.

    • Where do I go to register a motor vehicle?

      Register a car, truck, trailer, or motorcycle at any Satellite City Hall.

    • When will I get my personalized license plate?

      It takes 60 to 90 days to receive the plates.

    • How long is the grace period for registration of an out-of-state vehicle?

      The grace period is 10 working days.

    • How do I replace my license plate that has been damaged, lost, or stolen?

      To replace a damaged, lost, or stolen license plate, you can go to any Satellite City Hall.

    • How do I get a disabled parking permit?

      Disabled parking permits are available from the State of Hawai`i.

  • City Jobs

    • What employment benefits are offered by the City?

      Benefits include time off, health insurance, and retirement plan, among others.

    • What is the selection process for a job at the City?

      Apply online, submit necessary documents by mail or in person, and check your email for any notices. If you qualify, take written tests. Passing those, give interviews, and clear some medical examinations.

    • How do I apply to become a police officer?

      Go to honolulu.gov/hr during the application period to apply online for “Metropolitan Police Recruit.”

    • How do I find out about new job openings at the City?

      View current openings online, check the Honolulu Star-Advertiser Sunday newspaper, sign up for email notifications, or follow the Dept of Human Resources on Twitter and Facebook.

    • Is there an initial probation period after I'm hired by the city?

      City jobs have a 6-month probationary period.

    • Can I submit my resume instead of an application for City job openings?

      You must submit an online application to be considered for City job openings.

    • Can I apply for more than one City job at a time?

      You can apply for as many City jobs as you want.

  • Animals

    • What do I do about wild roosters?

      Call Animal Haven at (808) 799-7791.

    • How do I register my dog for a license tag?

      Fill out and submit an application form. Complete all the requirements needed on the form.

  • Traffic & Parking

    • What do I do if my car has been towed?

      If your car was towed from a public street, call Honolulu Police at 911.

    • How long will construction disrupt traffic on my street?

      To determine how long road construction will last, you need to contact the owner of the road.

    • Is there a schedule of street parades?

      Street parade schedules are posted by year and month.

  • Parks & Beaches

    • How far in advance can I reserve a camping site?

      Camping permits are available online starting 5 pm two Fridays before the Friday that you wish to camp.

    • Are pets allowed at the Botanical Gardens?

      Only service animals are allowed.

    • Do the Botanical Gardens charge an entry fee?

      All the Botanical Gardens on Oahu are free, except Foster Botanical Garden, which charges an admission fee.

    • Can I camp at the Botanical Gardens?

      You can camp at the Hoomaluhia Botanical Garden with a permit.

  • Environment

    • Who should I complain to about noise?

      To make a noise complaint, contact the appropriate agency responsible for the disturbance.

    • How can I learn more about Oahu's recycling and trash operations?

      The City's Tour de Trash program offers a series of free full-day tours of recycling and compost facilities.

    • How do I volunteer for a community clean-up?

      You can volunteer at the City's Adopt-A-Stream and Adopt-A-Block community clean-up programs.

    • How do I get rid of batteries, paints, and other household hazardous wastes?

      The City’s Department of Environmental Services can help residents get rid of household hazardous waste items safely

    • Where can I drop off recyclables?

      If you don’t have curbside recycling, most recyclable items can be taken to a City Multi-material Recycling Center or City Convenience Center.

  • Liquor Services

    • What is the minimum drinking age in Hawai`i?

      You have to be at least 21 to drink alcohol in Hawai`i.

    • How do I get a liquor employee registration card?

      Go to the Liquor Commission office with your TB clearance, current photo ID, proof of social security number, and payment.

    • Where can I take an alcohol server training class?

      At the City's Liquor Commission office at 711 Kapiolani Blvd, Suite 600, Honolulu.

  • Public Services

    • Where are the Satellite City Halls located?

      Satellite City Halls are located at Ala Moana, Fort St, Hawai`i Kai, Kailua, Kalihi-Kapalama, Kapolei, Pearlridge, Wahiawa, Waianae, and Windward City.

    • Where can I get free wifi in Honolulu?

      Find free wifi provided by the private sector in partnership with the City at honoluluhotzones.com

    • Can I speak with a live person at the City for help with a question?

      Call (808) 768-9311 or (808) 768-4385 to speak to a live person.

    • How do I qualify for Section 8 housing?

      To qualify for a Section 8 housing subsidy, you must meet all 7 eligibility requirements.

  • Emergencies

    • What do I do if I see a shark?

      To report a shark sighting, tell the Ocean Safety lifeguard on duty, or call 9-1-1

    • How do I get a copy of my ambulance report?

      Send a written request to the Emergency Medical Services Division of the City and County of Honolulu

  • Utilities

    • How do I request an extra green, blue, or gray curbside bin?

      Call the City's Refuse & Recycling Office at (808) 768-3200.

    • How do I find my trash, recycling, and bulky item collection schedules?

      You can find your trash, recyclable, green waste, and bulky item collection schedules by entering your address on the City’s Collection Schedules webpage.

    • How do I replace a broken trash cart?

      Call the Collections Services yard that services your neighborhood.

    • What can I place in my colored curbside trash and recycling bins?

      If you have curbside recycling, place recyclables in your blue bin, green waste in your green bin, and general household trash in the gray bin.

    • Who do I call for questions about my water or sewer bill?

      Call the Board of Water Supply for questions about your water bill, or the Dept of Environmental Services for questions on your sewer bill.

    • How are residential sewer charges calculated?

      Your monthly sewer charges are made up of a basic fixed charge for maintaining the sewer system, and a usage charge based on your water consumption.

    • Who do I contact if my trash or recycling cart was not picked up?

      Call the Collections Services yard that services your neighborhood.

    • How do I request a sewer bill credit for installing a new lawn?

      You can request a sewer fee credit for installing a new lawn by submitting an application to the Department of Environmental Services.

  • Taxes

    • How do I pay my property taxes?

      You can pay property taxes by mail or in person with cash, check, money order, or cashier’s check ― or electronically by credit card.

    • How do I apply for a homeowner’s tax credit?

      Fill out the Real Property Tax Credit for Homeowners application form (Form T-RPT100).

  • Construction Permit

    • Do I need to get a building permit to put up a fence?

      A building permit is required if the fence you want to build is 30 inches or taller.

    • How do I get in touch with a building inspector?

      You can call the number listed on your building permit for general construction work, or have your contractor contact the inspector named on an electrical or plumbing building permit.

  • Education

    • How old does my child need to be to get into kindergarten in public school?

      Your child must be 5 years old to enter kindergarten in Hawai`i public school.

  • Family Life

    • How do I apply for a civil union license?

      Apply for a civil union license with the State of Hawai`i by filling out an application form, paying a fee, and meeting with an agent who will verify your information.
